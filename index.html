web developer : designer

Elisha Hansen Schwabauer

Durham: Studying Front-End Engineering at The Iron Yard was an awesome experience. For 12 intense weeks I spent 70-80 hours a week studying HTML5, CSS3, & JavaScript. The process of web development fascinates me in an entirely new way. It intrigues me that so much depends on zeros & ones, that we've built a culture of technology that has shifted the way we communicate, & that to write code, one must learn how a computer processes information. I am excited about how much there is to learn, while striving to strike a balance between using logic with creativity in my career. It's an incredible process to build something that joins people all over the world.

Durham: Before The Iron Yard, I was studying design & hand lettering, working as a store artist. Of all the responsibilities I had in that position, what I enjoyed the most was hand lettering on chalkboards, as well as preparing store signage in Illustrator. I tend to gravitate towards a minimalist design, while conscientiously considering fonts, shapes, & colors.

San Fransisco, Carrboro: I remember sitting in class one day with a decisive feeling about needing to learn how to draw. I went on to sell my illustrations at weekly farmer's markets, festivals in Northern California, & at The ArtsCenter in Carrboro, NC. Taking an idea, spending hours drawing lines & adding colors in order to tell a story brings out my playful nature. Drawing was what led me to study design; design was what led me to web development.

Columbus, Houston, Denver/Boulder, San Fransisco: Prior to drawing, my background was in holistic health where I worked as a Massage Therapist, studied yoga & meditation. During this time I asked an abundance of questions about how muscles relate to bones, how breath relates to relaxation as well as strength, & how the mind can be tamed with sincere practice. What I enjoyed most about this work was observing how people transform.
